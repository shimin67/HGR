Brain-inspired Hybrid Generative Replay for Robotic Lifelong Learning

Anonymous Authors
Paper Code

Abstract

Robotic lifelong learning requires agents to continually acquire new manipulation skills while retaining previously learned ones, balancing plasticity and stability across sequential tasks. However, existing replay-based methods are limited by either high storage cost for experience replay or low replay fidelity for generative replay, while continual reinforcement learning remains unstable and sample-inefficient. This work presents a brain-inspired lifelong learning architecture based on hybrid generative knowledge replay (HGR) for robotic manipulation. To maintain replay fidelity and diversity while managing memory usage, HGR introduces a past-task fused-knowledge construction scheme that combines diffusion model-based trajectory generation, representative subset storage, and task-specific knowledge integration. A task-aware replay prioritization mechanism dynamically selects critical trajectories for replay to further mitigate forgetting. To balance policy plasticity and stability, we employ a dual-memory system, where a general policy built on the Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks (KANs) supports adaptation to new tasks while preserving previously acquired knowledge. Results on simulated robotic manipulation tasks demonstrate that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance by using less than 50% of replay storage while improving the success rate by over 10%. Real-world robotic arm manipulation experiments further validate its practical effectiveness.

Architecture

This work presents a brain-inspired robotic lifelong learning architecture based on hybrid episodic and generative knowledge replay for sequential manipulation tasks. It is designed to preserve past-task knowledge under limited memory while supporting stable integration of new manipulation skills.

  • We design a memory-efficient hybrid replay strategy that preserves previous knowledge by fusing stored real trajectories with generated ones. This design is inspired by replay-driven memory consolidation in biological systems, where the brain reactivates episodic traces and reconstructs fragments to support long-term cortical consolidation.
  • We further adopt a dual-stage learning system that mimics the brain's wake–sleep cycle, alternating between online skill acquisition and offline knowledge consolidation. Specifically, an immediate policy acquires new skills through online reinforcement learning, while a general policy with a KAN backbone consolidates knowledge through offline behavior cloning.

Results

Overall performance comparison on Continual World. Our model maintains stable policy updates and reaches an average success rate of approximately 93.4% on CW10 after sequentially learning all ten tasks, which outperforms the compared lifelong learning baselines.

Memory-performance tradeoff of hybrid generative replay. Compared with episodic replay, our hybrid replay mechanism selectively stores representative real trajectories and reduces replay memory consumption by more than 50% while maintaining superior lifelong learning performance. These results indicate that the proposed hybrid replay mechanism achieves a better tradeoff between replay storage and lifelong learning performance.

Quality and diversity evaluation of hybrid replay knowledge. We evaluate the quality and diversity of generated trajectories compared to pure generative replay. The hybrid episodic-generative replay achieves lower FID scores and larger trajectory distribution coverage, as well as improved precision-recall results. These results demonstrate that the hybrid architecture enables the diffusion model to generate more diverse and precise pseudo trajectories..
